Design Process



Observation

Location:

UTC Shopping Center

Situation:

Guardians push their stroller with their child inside or along side it, as they browse around in a shopping center together.

Example Of Observations

  • Both mother and child were pushing a stroller with the mother’s hand gripping the top of her child’s hand while the child was pushing the stroller, they were looking and moving their body towards the ice rink

  • Baby stroller were parked outside of children’s play area as the guardians supervise the child/children, while the stroller were left unmonitored.

  • Father tucks in sleeping child in baby stroller

Observed Needs

User needs to ...

  • Check in on their child
  • Entertain/distract their child
  • See when it gets dark
  • Protect their luggage
Solutions To Needs
  • Live monitoring camera inside the stroller with infrared vision
  • Built-in bluetooth speakers inside stroller
  • LEDs that project light around the stroller
  • Electronically lockable storage compartment under stroller

Design Decisions
Color:

I chose the colors dark purple and beige because they strongly contrast each other. Additionally, purple is a gender neutral color, applicable to both baby boys and girls.

Repetition:

All text colors are white, and the card layout are consistent with one another.

Proximity:

Individual controls of the strollers are grouped by their own respective cards.

Alignment:

Contents that can be appropriately aligned to the left are left aligned, to emphasize the line of alignment which enhances readability.

Overlay for disconnect action

Helps minimize disconnect error by requesting for confirmation.

Simplified home page with all controls located in one place:

Improves the accessibility of the stroller controls.

Labels on control cards:

Signify function of controls

Use of world knowledge for lock:

Lock color is red, unlock color is green. Helps the user easily identify the state of the lock.
